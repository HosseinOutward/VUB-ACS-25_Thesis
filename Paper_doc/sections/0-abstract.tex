\begin{abstract}
We present a learned distributed source coding (DSC) approach to reduce gradient communication cost in synchronous federated learning; The motivation is the escalating communication cost in distributed deep learning, especially with the rise of huge models like LLMs and Vit. At a high level, worker updates are quantized with a learned layered Wyner–Ziv (WZ) scheme, and the server exploits short-term side information from all past reconstruct from all workers. The goal is to convert cross-worker (and temporal) redundency into bit savings while preserving model accuracy.

Information-theoretic results in Slepian–Wolf (lossless) and Wyner–Ziv (lossy) show that correlated sources can be coded near conditional limits, implying substantial potential rate reductions when side information is available.

Prior works in model/gradient compression—classical and learned—typically rely on basic quantization and sparsification; parameter-wise WZ quantization has been largely unexplored at scale due to the complexity of classical constructions. In this paper we use reconstructed updates from other workers as side information, and offer a protocol for practical implementation of learned WZ coding for per-coordinate quantization. Out fininding position learned DSC as a scalable, drop-in communication primitive for FL and large-scale distributed training.
\end{abstract}
