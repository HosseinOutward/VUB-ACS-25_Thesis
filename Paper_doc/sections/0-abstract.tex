\begin{abstract}

 We address the growing communication bottleneck in synchronous federated and distributed deep learning, intensified by large models such as LLMs and ViTs. We present a learned distributed source coding (DSC) method that quantizes worker updates using a learned successive Wyner–Ziv (WZ) scheme. The server aggregates short-horizon side information from recent reconstructions across all workers, allowing the encoder to convert cross-worker and temporal redundancy into bit-rate savings while preserving model quality.
Our approach is grounded in Slepian–Wolf and Wyner–Ziv limits for correlated sources, which motivate coding near conditional rates when side information is available. In contrast to past works that rely on uniform quantization or sparsification, we introduce: (i) a multi-terminal per-coordinate learned WZ quantizer with refinement stages, (ii) a protocol applying said quantizer in a standard FL training loop.
Our findings position learned intra-worker DSC quantization as a scalable communication primitive for federated learning and large-scale distributed training.
\end{abstract}
