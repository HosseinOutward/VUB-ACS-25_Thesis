\emph{Experimental Setup.}
The model used in FL is ResNet-18 trained on SVHN dataset, including 5 workers, 80 communication rounds, FedSGD aggregation method, and 20 epochs of local training per client, all working with SGD optimizer with a learning rate of 0.001 and a batch size of 5000.

The learned quantizers are 3 stages with progressively decreasing symbols per stage to a minimum of 2, and an extra 1 bit setup using 1 stage and 2 symbols.
The learned non-WZ quantizer used in initialization is 2 stages with 16 symbols each.
Maximum number of reconstructions kept per worker is 10. The training of the quantizers is done with Adam optimizer with a learning rate of 0.001, weight decay of 1e-4, and scheduler with step size 45 and gamma of 0.3. The batch size is 10,000, training on a random subset of 300,000 samples per epoch, for 60 epochs. The initial Softmax-Gumbel temperature is set to 1.3 and the reconstruction loss weight, lambda, is set to 400.

Note that the bit capacity of the quantizer models does not always correspond to the actual number of bits used. For example, the quantizer used in initialization has a capacity of 256 bins but only uses around 20.

Due to complexity, the step requiring Slepian-Wolf coding is replaced with a joint entropy coder which when given the conditional prior \(p(u|y)\) will approximate the performance of Slepian-Wolf coding \cite{rANS}.

\subsection{Rate-Distortion Performance}
Figure \ref{fig:rate_distortion} shows the rate-distortion performance of the different communication protocols, including the proposed WZ quantizer, a learned non-WZ quantizer and the sign quantizer. To keep the comparison fair, we include the SW coding step in all protocols, with the sign protocol getting a perfect prior which is not possible in practice.

The WZ boundary was calculated using the Blahut–Arimoto method which gives an empirical lower bound on the rate-distortion performance. The SI used for this calculation is SI at round 25 which is slightly before overfitting starts to happen.

We see that our proposed WZ quantizer outperforms all other quantizers in terms of rate-distortion performance. Interestingly, the rate-distortion performance improves as the rounds of training progress. We believe the reason for that is increased temporal correlation since smaller updates are aggregations of overfitting workers leading to similar changes from round to round.
