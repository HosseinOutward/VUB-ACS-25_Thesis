\noindent\textbf{Experimental setup.}
We train a ResNet-18 on SVHN in a 5-worker FL setting for 80 rounds using FedSGD aggregation and 20 local epochs per client. Optimization is SGD (learning rate \(10^{-3}\), batch size \(5000\)). The learned Wyner--Ziv quantizer (WZQ) uses 3 stages with progressively fewer symbols per stage (down to 2). For warmup/initialization we use a non-WZ quantizer with 2 stages and 16 symbols per stage. We retain at most 10 past reconstructions per worker as side information. Quantizers are trained with Adam (learning rate \(10^{-3}\), weight decay \(10^{-4}\)) and a step scheduler (step size \(45\), \(\gamma=0.3\)); batch size \(10{,}000\); for 60 epochs, each epoch sampling a random subset of \(300{,}000\) examples. The initial Gumbel--Softmax temperature is \(1.3\), and the reconstruction-loss weight is \(\lambda=400\).

Note that the nominal symbol \emph{capacity} need not equal the symbols actually used; e.g., the initialization quantizer has capacity \(256\) symbols yet activates only 20.

For coding complexity, we replace the explicit Slepian--Wolf (SW) stage with a joint entropy coder that, given \(p(u\,|\,y)\), closely approximates SW performance~\cite{rANS}.

\subsection{Rate--Distortion Performance}
Figure~\ref{fig:rate_distortion} compares (i) the proposed WZQ, (ii) a learned non-WZ quantizer, and (iii) a sign quantizer. We also report on a protocol variant \emph{without} realignment phases. To ensure fairness, all methods include the SW coding approximation step; the sign baseline is given a perfect (oracle) prior, which is idealized and not achievable in practice. The Wyner--Ziv (WZ) bound is computed via Blahut--Arimoto using side information from round 25.

Our WZQ dominates the alternatives, and its rate--distortion curve improves over training rounds---likely because model updates shrink and become more similar across rounds for the same worker, resetting them in a way each round (e.g., aggregation moderates overfitting workers). The variant without realignment degrades, highlighting the benefit of realignment.

\subsection{FL Performance}
Figure~\ref{fig:fl_performance} reports FL accuracy and per-round communication cost, including protocol variant with a post-warmup bit allowance of 1-bit for quantization instead of the 8-bit budget (3 stages with 2 bins). We also include a training report without any compression, which serves as an upper bound.

The trained AUC is nearly perfect and outperforms the sign in both accuracy and communication cost. The 1-bit variant performs essentially as well.
In both protocols, The rate generally increases until retraining, then becomes lower and more stable toward the end, probably due to the aforementioned shrinking updates and resetting.
