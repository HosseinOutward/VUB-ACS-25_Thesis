\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We present a novel gradient quantization approach for federated learning leveraging inter-agent redundancy.
Our method exploits cross-worker and temporal correlations through learned successive multi-terminal Wyner-Ziv quantization, achieving superior rate-distortion performance while maintaining model accuracy.
The approach is particularly effective for scenarios with many agents and large models where inter-agent redundancy benefits are pronounced.
Our results demonstrate the potential of such techniques to significantly reduce communication overhead.

Future work could integrate our method with post-quantization compression techniques like vector quantization.
