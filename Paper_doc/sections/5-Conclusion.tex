\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We have presented a novel approach to quantization of gradients in federated learning.
Our method leverages the redundancy between agent updates to quantize the individual parameters more effectively.
We have shown that our method improves upon traditional and even learned quantization techniques in terms of rate-distortion performance while maintaining model accuracy.
Our approach is particularly effective in scenarios with a large number of agents and large models, where the benefits of exploiting inter-agent redundancy are most pronounced.
Overall, our work demonstrates the potential of learned successive multi-terminal Wyner-Ziv quantization for improving communication efficiency in federated learning, paving the way for more scalable and efficient distributed learning systems.

Future work could explore the integration of our method with other compression techniques which follow after quantization, such as vector quantization.
