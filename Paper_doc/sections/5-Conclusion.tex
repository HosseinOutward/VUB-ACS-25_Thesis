\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We have presented a novel approach to quantization of model updates for use in federated learning. Our method leverages the redundency between agents update to quantize the individual parameters more effectively. We have shown that our method improves upon traditional and even learned quantization techniques in terms of rate-distortion performance while maintaining model accuracy. Our approach is particularly effective in scenarios with a large number of agents, and large models, where the benefits of exploiting inter-agent redundancy are most pronounced.

Future work could explore the integration of our method with other compression techniques which follow after quantization, such as pruning, sparsification and vector quantization.
Additionally a better mixing of temporal quantization with the inter-agent quantization could lead to further improvements.
Additionally, the use of such techniques for fedAVG and also transfer of model to agents could be explored.

Overall, our work demonstrates the potential of learned successive multi-terminal Wyner-Ziv quantization for improving communication efficiency in federated learning, paving the way for more scalable and efficient distributed learning systems.
