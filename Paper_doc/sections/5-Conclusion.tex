\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented a learned distributed source coding quantization approach for federated learning that exploits cross-worker and temporal correlations through a multi-terminal learned Wyner-Ziv quantizer with successive refinement. Our method achieves substantial rate reductions compared to conventional gradient compression techniques while maintaining model accuracy.

The proposed protocol effectively balances the trade-off between exploiting inter-node correlation and avoiding error accumulation through periodic realignment phases. Experimental results on ResNet-18/SVHN demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach, showing superior rate-distortion performance compared to learned non-WZ quantizers and sign quantization baselines.

Future work could integrate our method with post-quantization techniques, such as vector quantization.
