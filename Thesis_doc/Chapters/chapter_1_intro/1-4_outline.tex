\section{Outline of the Thesis}

The remainder of this thesis is organized as follows: Chapter 2 provides a comprehensive review of the relevant literature, covering the basics of information theory, DSC, and distributed/federated learning, as well as relevant works done by other researchers.

Chapter 3 covers the methodology chosen, including the training of a layered WZ quantizer, pre- and post-processing of the accumulated gradients, and outlines the main compression protocols used.

Chapter 4 presents the quantitative results from each of the protocols in a structured manner and provides an in-depth discussion of these results, interpreting their significance.

Finally, Chapter 5 concludes the thesis by summarizing the key findings and offering directions for future research.
