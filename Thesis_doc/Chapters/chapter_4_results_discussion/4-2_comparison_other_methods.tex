\section{Comparison with Other Methods}
It is somewhat difficult to compare our method with other methods in the literature, as we only have results for a specific model and dataset, while other methods might have been tested on different models and datasets with different starting conditions. However, we can still make some general observations.

The process proposed by \cite{ZhangTao2022} shows that their closest comparable model, the ResNet-20 trained on SVHN, takes 20 epochs to improve from 61\% to 83\% accuracy and claims to save 47\% in communication costs compared to a non-DSC signSGD method, which appears to use $1.8 \times 10^6$ bits. Considering a parameter count of 270k and 30 rounds and 10 worker, the bits per parameter (BPP) per round would be approximately 0.35 (non-DSC sits at 0.66).

For our configuration, which is similar only with regard to the dataset used, the BPP is 0.9 and we achieve higher accuracy of 91\% in 20 epochs, starting from 60\% accuracy. This is expected as we have a much higher percision (1 sign bit vs n smartly positioned bins).
If their gradients are anything similar to ours, their MAPE distortion only for the signSGD method would be around 1200\%, much higher than our ~20\% MAPE distortion.

We could not find any other work that uses the SVHN dataset with ResNet-18 and reports on DSC compression rates for direct comparison.
